The road here

A career spent where systems meet the human in the bed

Robert's path is unusual on purpose. He trained as a physician in the Philippines, ran a hospital on a former US naval base, managed healthcare operations for thirteen years, co-founded a natural healing institute, worked in AI adoption consulting and business development — and then chose to spend more than two decades as a critical care nurse in the United States, at the bedside, on nights, in the rooms where systems succeed or fail one patient at a time.

That combination — the physician's clinical lens, the administrator's systems view, and the nurse's hands — is why Nurse AI OS looks the way it does. It's built by someone who has signed the budget, written the policy, and held the hand of the patient the policy was written for.

What he's building

One ecosystem, five instruments

Each organization answers a different question — who sets the standards, who decides, what enforces the ethics, where nurses gather, and what carries the work. Together they form a single architecture: nurse-led AI governance, from bedside to institution.

Governance authority

NAIO Institute

The Nurse AI Orchestration Institute defines standards, develops credentialing, and builds compliance frameworks for AI workforce systems in healthcare — working toward becoming the FAA of healthcare AI: no agent operating without registration, risk classification, and an assigned human steward.

Core principle: governance before autonomy.

Orchestration control plane

Florence-X

Mission control for AI agent workforces: dispatches tasks, enforces governance policies, routes models and tools, manages memory boundaries, and coordinates agents as a unified workforce — on sovereign, owned infrastructure rather than cloud dependency.

Humans retain final decision authority at every layer.

The conscience layer

EDENA

Ethical Design & Enablement for Nursing Augmentation — classifies, gates, and audits every AI action before execution. Ethics as executable infrastructure: AI may execute workflows, but humans remain responsible for judgment.

🟢 Green — low risk, may proceed🟡 Yellow — conditional approval🔴 Red — human authorization mandatory

The governed agent operating system

Nurse AI OS

The system you're on right now — the NAIO-governed operating layer where the whole architecture becomes personal. It sits beneath Florence-X mission control, applies EDENA risk classification and governed memory, and leverages the open-source Hermes engine so complex workflows become evolving, reusable skills that compound across tasks and seasons.

Florence-X decides and governs. Nurse AI OS routes, enforces, and personalizes. Hermes learns and adapts. Nurses steward all of it.

Strategic initiative

13 World Projects for Nurse Empowerment

The long-horizon agenda — deliberately ambitious, deliberately governed. Grouped by what each protects.

Governance & constitutional (Projects 1–3)
01 · Nightingale Protocol — a global nurse AI constitutional convention that drafts, ratifies, and enforces binding governance standards.
02 · EDENA Sentient Shield — evolving EDENA into a real-time boundary-enforcement engine that catches confidence drift and scope creep before they reach a patient.
03 · Nurse Data Cooperative & Ethics Council — nurses collectively controlling how de-identified clinical data trains AI, overseen by frontline nurses, patients, and communities.
Infrastructure & sovereignty (Projects 4–6)
04 · Florence Sovereign Mesh — nurse-controlled distributed compute, so governance never depends on Big Tech permission.
05 · Judgment Vault & Knowledge Graph — capturing the invisible cognitive work of nursing as structured, citable, trainable knowledge.
06 · Compassion Engine — constraint architecture ensuring advanced AI preserves therapeutic presence and relational care, with compassion as a measurable governance requirement.
Clinical & safety (Projects 7–8)
07 · CAIRLN & Guardian Commons — a nurse-led AI incident-reporting system modeled on aviation's ASRS: near-misses, failures, and safety signals becoming a shared safety-intelligence commons.
08 · Simulation & ICU Co-Pilot — testing AI tools under worst-case conditions (3 a.m., short-staffed, high-acuity) — where they actually break — before they reach real units.
Workforce & pipeline (Projects 9–10)
09 · Conductor & Builder Guild (NINE²) — the incubator turning nurse-led AI ideas into governed, operational products through cohort-based founder development.
10 · Context Bundles & Mentor Network — productized knowledge artifacts with provenance baked in, distributed through a global chapter-and-mentorship network.
Global & equity (Projects 11–13)
11 · Philippine Bridge — sovereign AI access for Global South nurses, transforming brain drain into distributed governance leadership.
12 · Climate-Health Sentinel Network — nurse-led climate intelligence that anticipates climate-driven health threats instead of only responding to them.
13 · Nurse Robotics Governance Authority — governance standards for AI-powered robots entering clinical environments, so when physical AI arrives, nurses already own the oversight architecture.
