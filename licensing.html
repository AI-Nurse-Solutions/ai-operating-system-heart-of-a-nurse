Licensing, identity, and accountability

Open platform. Protected identity. Governed healthcare implementation.

The software is open, the commercial product name is protected, and healthcare deployment remains under human accountability.

The software is open

The Nurse AI OS source code is licensed under the Apache License 2.0. You may use it, study it, modify it, distribute it, and deploy it commercially without asking permission or paying a license fee, subject to the license terms. The license includes an explicit patent grant.

Documentation, playbooks, and curricula are licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0. You may use, adapt, translate, and teach from them with attribution.

Third-party components retain their upstream licenses and notices. Hermes Agent is used as an external runtime, and OpenClaw is referenced for interoperability and architectural research; neither codebase is vendored in this repository as of July 14, 2026. The project publishes current distribution status and attribution in THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md.

The commercial product name is protected

Nurse AI OS™ is a trademark of Robert Domondon. An application to register the mark is pending; no claim of federal registration is made.

You do not need permission to state truthfully that your product is built with, compatible with, or forked from Nurse AI OS. You may review, compare, critique, parody, or report on the project. The trademark will not be asserted against good-faith criticism.

The policy prevents deception: calling an unofficial distribution the official product, using the logo without permission, implying an endorsement that does not exist, or issuing unofficial Nurse AI OS certifications. Read the complete policy in TRADEMARKS.md.

Healthcare deployment is governed

Openness applies to the code. Discipline applies to deployment. A licensed clinician remains accountable for every clinical decision. The software takes no autonomous diagnostic or treatment action, and raw protected health information must not be placed in agent memory, telemetry, logs, or model context.

Educational, administrative, and clinical functions must remain visibly distinct. Any clinical implementation requires the deploying institution's own validation, legal and regulatory review, information-security approval, and clinical-governance approval before it touches care.

Nurse AI OS is workforce, administrative, and educational infrastructure. It is not a medical device and is not cleared, approved, or validated for diagnosis, treatment, or autonomous clinical decision-making. Read the binding requirements for official distributions in SAFETY.md.

The model: open software, protected commercial identity, and governed healthcare implementation. Agents propose. Humans judge. Nurses steward.

Read all licenses and policies in the public repository. To request logo use or certification-program participation, or to report suspected misuse of the mark, contact the trademark holder.
