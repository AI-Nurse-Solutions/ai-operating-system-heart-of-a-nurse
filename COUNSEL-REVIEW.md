# Counsel Review Queue

**Status:** planning checklist only; not operative legal terms, not legal advice, and not a substitute for counsel-drafted agreements.

## Already covered — do not duplicate

- Keep `LICENSE` as the unmodified standard Apache License 2.0. Its Sections 7 and 8 already provide the code warranty disclaimer and limitation of liability.
- Do not add supplementary liability terms to `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, or repository policy files.
- Documentation remains under CC BY 4.0, including its Section 5 disclaimer framework.
- Keep medical and deployment boundaries in `SAFETY.md`; do not turn that engineering-governance document into a liability waiver.

## 1. Trademark policy and filing review

Counsel review of `TRADEMARKS.md`, the pending application language, goods-and-services scope, permitted-use examples, certification-mark treatment, enforcement posture, and the later entity-assignment path.

## 2. Hosted-service Terms of Use

Counsel-drafted terms for any service operated by the project, separate from the open-source license. Required subjects:

- service warranty disclaimer;
- limitation of liability;
- user indemnification for user-caused misuse, prohibited conduct, rights violations, or breach;
- prohibited clinical use and prohibited PHI upload;
- suspension, termination, and incident-response rights;
- governing law, venue, dispute process, and any required consumer terms; and
- alignment with the privacy policy, data-processing terms, and actual service architecture.

**Allocation principle:** liability follows authority. The terms may allocate responsibility for harm users cause, but should not disclaim all operator liability for product defects or reduce every operator-caused loss to a contract-value cap. Counsel should draft exceptions and caps that match the authority and risk each party actually controls.

## 3. Enterprise and certification agreements

Use negotiated agreements—not repository terms—for enterprise deployment, certification, badge licensing, insurance minimums, indemnity, audit rights, incident duties, data processing, and liability allocation. Terms should be tailored per contract and remain consistent with the project's Insurance–Liability–Indemnity Doctrine.
