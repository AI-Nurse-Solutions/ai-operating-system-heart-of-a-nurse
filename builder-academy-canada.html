Strategic thesis

The next global AI builders do not need permission from Silicon Valley.

AI has changed who can build. Small, high-agency teams can now create useful tools, prototype faster, and reach global communities from where they are. NAIO applies that shift to healthcare education with a stricter rule: build quickly, but govern before clinical proximity.

For Canada, the opportunity is not merely to commercialize AI research. It is to prepare Canadian nurses, educators, students, and nurse leaders to become AI supervisors, workflow designers, agent builders, founders, and ethical stewards.

Canada implementation

Canada: nursing schools, associations, and informatics communities first

The Canada lane begins with nursing education because schools, faculty, and deans shape the next generation of nurses. Associations, nursing informatics communities, nurse founders, and Canada’s AI ecosystem add trust, technical depth, and pathways into responsible innovation.

1. Nursing schools

Recruit deans, program chairs, faculty champions, and student leaders into briefings, listening sessions, and no-PHI builder sprints.

2. Associations + informatics

Invite nurse leaders, provincial communities, and nursing informatics reviewers into roundtables on stewardship, governance, and practical workflow support.

3. AI ecosystem bridge

Connect with Vector, Mila, Amii, CIFAR/CAISI-aligned contacts, and innovation communities as responsible-AI collaborators — not as endorsement claims.

Minimum viable proof

The first offer is a 7-day no-PHI builder sprint.

Do not start with a massive incubator. Start with one governed build loop that proves Canadian participants can create useful artifacts safely.

1

Orient

AI shift, NAIO purpose, EDENA boundaries, and Hermes–OpenClaw cloud workspace concept.

2

Build

One public-safe artifact using AI coding agents, templates, and synthetic or public data only.

3

Review

Complete an EDENA card: purpose, data used, risk tier, human reviewer, stop condition, and next safe version.

4

Showcase

Publish only approved demo cards. No patient data, no clinical claims, no school logo without permission.

Safe prototype examples

  • Nursing study planner
  • Faculty lesson-plan assistant
  • Public article research board
  • Nurse manager meeting brief template
  • Fake-data operations dashboard
  • Prototype landing page for a nurse-led AI venture

Blocked: diagnosis, triage, treatment, medication advice, patient-specific recommendations, EHR screenshots, charts, lab results, identifiable stories, autonomous patient/family communication, or clinical validation claims.

Before Robert visits

2026 outreach sequence

The visit should not be cold. The goal is to arrive with warm contacts, named champions, sprint results, and a small steward council already forming.

Phase 1 · Authority warm-up

Publish this page, build the interest list, identify first 40 contacts, and invite 5–7 trusted Canadian advisors.

Phase 2 · Listening roundtables

Run online sessions for nursing education, informatics, nurse founders, and workflow automation. Capture privacy, equity, bilingual, rural/northern, and approval concerns.

Phase 3 · First sprint

Recruit 20–30 participants, run the 7-day no-PHI sprint, and select 3–5 safe demo cards.

Phase 4 · Institution recruitment

Share results with deans and associations; ask for pilot conversations and named faculty champions.

Phase 5 · Visit conversion

Meet warm contacts, host a small stewardship salon, and secure letters of interest for 2027 pilots.

Safety boundary

This is not clinical AI deployment.

This academy is for education, personal productivity, workflow mapping, public-safe prototypes, founder learning, and non-PHI community building. It is not a clinical decision system. It does not diagnose, triage, treat, prescribe, validate clinical competence, or authorize deployment in a school, hospital, clinic, or public-health program.

No PHI. No patient-specific advice. No endorsement claims. No certification claims. No autonomous clinical operations.
