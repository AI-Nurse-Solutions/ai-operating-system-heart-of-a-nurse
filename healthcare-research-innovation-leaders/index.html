Healthcare Research and Innovation Lead

Identity, authority, and safety boundary

A leadership lane is not institutional authority

DISCOVER uses one isolated healthcare_research_innovation_leadership lane, the research_innovation_discover.* namespace, and one canonical My DISCOVER Mission Control. It can serve nurse leaders and interdisciplinary healthcare research or innovation leaders without merging their underlying state into another population.

Selection verifies nothing. A title, credential, employer, technical access, earlier approval, download, or AI capability does not establish identity, delegated mandate, data rights, IRB/HRO/QI status, regulatory or legal authority, scientific validity, safety, funding, intellectual-property rights, publication rights, or permission to deploy.

DISCOVER is separate from the Nurse AI OS Steward’s Charter, the hospital-administrator STEWARD governance preview, Medical Resident ROUNDS, and Respiratory Care BREATHE. The parent Nurse AI OS may receive only product/version, coarse state, and a deep link after explicit approval—not questions, study titles, partners, findings, IP/funding details, private goals, memory, permissions, or traces.

Live clinical concerns, participant-safety issues, adverse events, complaints, and emergencies leave DISCOVER immediately for official local procedures and authorized humans. DISCOVER never assesses seriousness, relatedness, expectedness, reportability, urgency, diagnosis, or care.

Four control frameworks

Questions, evidence, experiments, and agents stay bounded

FRAME

Boundary before determination. Facts and purpose; Risk, rights, review and responsible owner; Activity and interaction; Materials, methods and data; Endpoint and intended use.

It names possible pathways and unresolved questions. It never issues a research, QI, clinical, regulatory, or product determination.

TRACE

Evidence with provenance. Thesis; Reference; Authority; Context; Exceptions, evolution and expiry.

Sources, interpretations, limits, conflicts, corrections, retractions, applicability, reviewers, and next-review dates remain visible.

PROBE

Reversible innovation. Human problem and non-goals; Rights, risk and burden; Options including nontechnology; Bounded non-production test; Evaluation, exit and rollback.

No live patient-facing experiment, procurement, production connection, or scale action follows automatically.

ORBIT

Control every software agent. Objective and owner; Role, rights and restricted data; Bounds; Inspection of frozen hashes; Test, human decision, reconciliation, kill and retirement.

No self-activation, recursive delegation, permission widening, hidden retry, background execution, or approval power.

Mission and impact, unmet need, portfolio choices, and research–QI–clinical–innovation boundary questions without locking in a solution or erasing burden.

E + R — Evidence, Responsible Innovation & Renewal

Horizon scans, implementation and adoption evaluation, dissemination integrity, bounded agent charters, leadership development, and an optional owner-private whole-life plan.

Governed installation

Download ≠ installation

  1. Use a new or verified-isolated lane. A shared, collision-prone, or inadequately separated target is a blocker.
  2. Give Hermes the entire one-file program. Modular components are for inspection only; do not install them individually.
  3. Authorize inspection only. Issue INSPECT DISCOVER INSTALLER ONLY — CREATE NO STATE.
  4. Review one exact combined DISCOVER Activation Card. It must bind target, complete-program digest, authority, routes, partitions, data/actions, capabilities, all inactive/disabled states, burden, receipts, stop conditions, rollback, purge, and removal.
  5. Approve the exact post-card command, revise, install foundation only, or cancel. Silence, timeout, title, access, download, or a prior general request is never approval.
  6. After approval only: S0 → foundation/core → 40 S1 criteria → inactive DISCOVER overlay → Mission Control → 120 S2 criteria → Activation Report.
No institutional authority by download. Organizational deployment requires a separately provisioned approved environment, verified authority and governing determinations, approved systems and agreements, data rights, privacy/security review, official records, retention/deletion, incident routes, reconciliation, expiry, and local authorization. This release creates none of those.

The 160 items are evidence-bearing target-runtime criteria in specified state. Repository validation proves their inventory, deterministic variants, canonical artifacts, and package contract. It does not prove they ran in a target Hermes environment and is not clinical validation, compliance evidence, accreditation, certification, IRB/HRO/QI determination, institutional approval, scientific-validity proof, safety proof, or outcomes evidence.
