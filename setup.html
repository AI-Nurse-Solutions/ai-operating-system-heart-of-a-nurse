The rules that protect everyone

Plain-language safety rules

You memorized the rights of medication administration. These are shorter, and they protect your patients, your license, and you.

No patient data. Ever.

No names, charts, screenshots, room numbers, record numbers, dates, or stories that could identify a patient — in any AI tool, at any level of this program. If you're unsure whether something counts: it counts. Leave it out.

AI drafts. You decide.

Every AI output is a draft until a human reviews it. Nothing gets sent, published, installed, or acted on without your judgment. Human gates are checkpoints, not suggestions.

Your license is not outsourceable.

No AI here supports clinical decisions, assessments, or patient-specific care questions. The system carries your busywork — never your judgment, never your accountability.

Employer systems stay at work.

No EHR screens, no internal policies marked confidential, no staff or student identifiable data, no workplace secrets. Use public, fictional, de-identified, or personal material only — unless your organization's compliance team has explicitly approved a tool and workflow.

When in doubt, ask a human.

Bring the gray areas to the Lamp Huddle or email great.ai.nurses@gmail.com. A question asked early beats an incident reported late.

Plain-language test before entering anything sensitive: if you cannot name the approved environment, the data type, the AI feature, and the accountable human owner — don't put it in the tool.

These rules have a name: EDENA

The five rules above are the plain-language version of EDENA — Ethical Design & Enablement for Nursing Augmentation — the governance framework enshrined in every layer of Nurse AI OS. AI may carry workflows; humans remain responsible for judgment. Every action you ever automate gets classified before it runs:

🟢 Green — low risk, may proceed 🟡 Yellow — conditional, you approve first 🔴 Red — human authorization mandatory, always

You don't need to memorize this — the roadmap tags every task with its tier, and you stay in Green and Yellow for your whole first month. You'll meet EDENA formally in Week 6 of the workbook, and on Day 25 of the roadmap you'll map your own tiers. By then it won't feel like a framework — it will feel like how you already think: assess the risk before you act, and keep a human hand on anything that matters.
