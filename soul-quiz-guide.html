Map the whole person—not just the badge.

The redesigned quiz builds a role constellation: primary, supporting, emerging, and contextual roles coordinated through one Core SOUL. It distinguishes current responsibility from developing capability, confidence, credentials, and aspiration.

The central idea

One identity can carry many congruent roles.

You may be a bedside nurse, preceptor, quality contributor, certification candidate, graduate student, entrepreneur, family caregiver, and wellness advocate at the same time. The quiz does not ask you to discard those identities. It asks how each one functions in this season.

Primary

The role currently holding the greatest share of attention, responsibility, or professional identity. Choose one current center of gravity.

Supporting

A role that regularly changes how you work, learn, lead, collaborate, or decide.

Emerging

A role or capability you are intentionally developing. It is not current authority.

Contextual

A role active only in a specific project, organization, assignment, environment, or season.

Primary does not mean only. It helps order attention. It is not a winning archetype, permanent label, or claim of authority.

How to answer

Keep seven different things separate.

FieldWhat to enterWhat it never proves
Current roleWhat you actually do or carry nowEmployment or appointment
AuthorizationHow scope, assignment, delegation, license, privilege, or supervision is definedThat the claim was verified here
Competence evidenceWhether capability is developing, supervised, practiced, or verified elsewhereIndependent competence
ConfidenceHow comfortable you feel todayCompetence, readiness, or authority
CredentialNot claimed, in progress, aspirational, or reportedCredential validity
Emerging capabilityWhat you are intentionally learning towardA current role or permission
AspirationA desired future identity or outcomePresent authority
When unsure, say so. Missing or contradictory information should remain uncertain until the appropriate human, program, employer, institution, or credentialing body clarifies it.

The quiz flow

A concise core, followed by only the modules you need.

Confirm stewardship boundaries

No PHI, no authority inference, no prohibited academic work, and accountable human judgment for consequential decisions.

Select the complete constellation

Choose every relevant clinical, learning, education, leadership, administration, operations, quality, research, technology, innovation, business, community, wellness, and advocacy role. Add a generic local role if needed.

Describe each role

Assign status and rate attention, responsibility, identity salience, and confidence separately. Declare authorization, competence-evidence, and credential posture without treating the quiz as verification.

Define the shared Core SOUL

Choose stable values, mission, people or systems served, motivations, strengths, work and learning styles, decision preferences, pressures, and wellness limits. Select the Personal, Professional, Community–Entrepreneurial, Side Gig, and Interest environments that should coordinate around that same identity.

Configure AI behavior

Select teacher, tutor, coach, mentor, research assistant, thought partner, collaborator, coordinator, analyst, advisor, governance monitor, or other modes. The quiz enforces minimum human-review floors.

Complete relevant deep dives

The browser adds one module per distinct role domain and the Advanced Studies overlay when active. Shared modules are shown once, even when several roles trigger them.

Review and download

Review your constellation, synergies, tensions, dashboards, agents, and governance boundaries. Download the Markdown bundle and optional naio-soul.json locally.

Student and frontline-support lane

Prelicensure student and Nursing Assistant roles may coexist—but are not interchangeable.

A person may be both a prelicensure nursing student and a Nursing Assistant, CNA, patient care technician, or healthcare assistant. The module records both while preserving different education pathways, employment duties, delegation, supervision, certifications, and authorized scope.

Student context

Program type and stage, clinical placement, simulation, supervised learning, entrance/progression, graduation, NCLEX, academic integrity, faculty and preceptor oversight.

Assistant/PCT context

Training or certification, employment setting, assigned responsibilities, delegated duties, authorized scope, chain of command, supervisor oversight, frontline caregiving contribution.

Never cross the streams. Assistant employment does not create student-nurse or licensed-nurse authority. Student clinical experience does not independently expand employment scope. AI may tutor, rehearse, plan, and reflect; it may not create unsupervised clinical authority, replace faculty or supervision, fabricate competence, or complete prohibited work.

Cross-role growth

Advanced Studies is an overlay—not another identity.

Activate Advanced Studies for certifications, recertification, continuing competency, degrees, residency or fellowship, formal specialization, leadership, education, research, informatics, AI, business, entrepreneurship, continuing education, or career transition.

The overlay connects the pathway to every role it serves. A nurse manager pursuing a doctorate may receive Unit Leadership and Operations, Advanced Studies and Certification, Graduate Degree and Research, Quality/Safety/Governance, and Wellness/Sustainability Mission Controls—all sharing one values system and capacity limit.

Study is real work, but it is not proof. Preparation stage, progress evidence, confidence, and gaps remain self-reported. AI may tutor and plan; it may not impersonate, fabricate, grade, certify, or complete prohibited work.

Examples

Two integrated profiles

Bedside nurse + preceptor + quality contributor + entrepreneur + CCRN candidate

  • Primary: Bedside Nurse
  • Supporting: Clinical Preceptor; Quality Improvement Specialist
  • Emerging: Nurse Entrepreneur
  • Contextual: Family Caregiver; Wellness Advocate
  • Advanced Studies: CCRN preparation

Likely dashboards: Bedside Clinical Command; Education and Mentorship; Quality, Safety, and Governance; Advanced Studies and Certification; Entrepreneurship and Business; Wellness and Personal Sustainability.

Prelicensure student + nursing assistant + emerging bedside nurse

  • Primary: Prelicensure Nursing Student
  • Supporting: Nursing Assistant/PCT
  • Emerging: Bedside Nurse
  • Contextual: Family Caregiver

Likely dashboards: Prelicensure Student and Nursing Assistant Mission Control; Clinical Learning, Skills, and Simulation; Education, Certification, and NCLEX Preparation when applicable; Work-School-Wellness Coordination.

Results and review

Multiple dashboards, one accountable person.

Every dashboard shares the Core SOUL, values, long-term mission, approved memory, governance boundaries, goals, commitments, learning priorities, and wellness limits. Each dashboard may have its own context, tasks, agents, tools, permissions, risk level, data boundary, human-review rule, and performance indicators. “Mission Control” is an organizational view—not a credential, competence finding, approval, command authority, or separate personality.

All quiz recommendations are A0 recommendation-only and EDENA not evaluated. Taking the quiz does not activate an agent, connector, memory rule, schedule, message, publication, transaction, or automation.

Review quarterly and at every material change. Update after role, assignment, supervision, credential, education, workload, caregiving, wellness, or boundary changes. Preserve prior exports until you have compared and approved the new version.
