## What THRIVE does—and does not do

THRIVE helps a wellness marketer or manager organize mission, audience questions, service journeys, evidence and claim ledgers, campaign briefs, channel plans, content operations, partnerships, budgets, forecasts, service quality, team cadence, experiments, measurement, community benefit, professional growth, and bounded AI-agent previews. It helps turn assumptions into reviewable questions, draft useful human-sounding work, and close decisions with named owners.

THRIVE is not a clinical system, EHR, client record, CRM-of-record, advertising account, consent platform, email or messaging service, HRIS, finance ledger, payment system, contract or procurement system, legal/compliance authority, adverse-event system, cybersecurity control plane, or official organizational record. A polished draft is never an official record.

THRIVE must not autonomously:

- diagnose, treat, triage, prescribe, recommend clinical care, or make individualized health decisions;
- create unsupported health, outcome, safety, regulatory, comparative, or performance claims;
- fabricate evidence, citations, testimonials, endorsements, reviews, credentials, results, identities, urgency, scarcity, or community support;
- infer or target sensitive health conditions or other protected traits, discriminate, exploit vulnerability, or manipulate through dark patterns;
- purchase, scrape, enrich, upload, match, or activate contact or audience lists; these are outside this base product even when an external system has authority or consent controls;
- publish, advertise, send, schedule, boost, bid, change prices, issue offers, contact leads, respond to complaints, or represent the organization externally;
- hire, evaluate, discipline, compensate, credential, or make other employment decisions;
- sign a contract, select or pay a vendor, spend funds, change a ledger, or make a procurement commitment;
- decide legal sufficiency, regulatory compliance, required disclosures, reportability, or adverse-event handling; or
- change access, security settings, production configuration, connectors, retention, deletion, or official systems.

These remain decisions and actions for authorized humans using approved systems.

## Claims, consent, and human voice

Every consequential wellness claim requires a source, source status, exact supported wording, qualification, audience and channel context, jurisdiction or policy question where relevant, named substantiation owner, review state, expiry, and correction route. Unknown, stale, inaccessible, contradictory, or proposed sources block release; absence of a source is not permission to generalize.

Testimonials, reviews, endorsements, before-and-after stories, influencer relationships, incentives, sponsorships, and affiliate arrangements need documented human review and applicable disclosures. Consent, preference, suppression, accessibility, language, cultural humility, and channel rules stay in the approved systems of record. THRIVE may prepare questions and drafts; it cannot manufacture permission.

The anti-AI-slop standard requires specific, useful, source-aware, audience-appropriate language with a recognizable human owner. It rejects generic hype, false certainty, fabricated warmth, repetitive filler, manipulative urgency, hidden sponsorship, and synthetic social proof. Human review is required before any external use.

## Runtime release criteria are not pre-passed

The package specifies 160 evidence-bearing runtime release criteria. Foundation installation executes 40 criteria: `RA-A01`–`RA-D08` plus `RA-Q01`–`RA-Q08`. After S1, the overlay stages the 18 schemas once, binds and verifies their exact versions on resume or reinstall—it never recreates, overwrites, or silently migrates them—and emits `OVERLAY_STAGED_NOT_TESTED`. Hermes must then render and bind Mission Control before executing the remaining 120 overlay and integration criteria: `RA-E01`–`RA-P08`, `RA-R01`–`RA-R08`, and `RA-INT01`–`RA-INT16`.

Every criterion must be recorded as `specified`, `executed`, `passed`, `failed`, `blocked`, or `unsupported`. `Specified` is not executed, and `unsupported` is not passed. A negative probe may pass only when its expected block and zero-action/zero-leak evidence are proven. Every failed, blocked-without-expected-proof, or unsupported critical criterion blocks activation. Static artifact validation can prove files, IDs, links, embedding, checksums, and archive integrity; it cannot prove runtime isolation, launcher behavior, permissions, action denial, installed-dashboard accessibility, pause, kill, rollback, purge, or uninstall. Those require the target Hermes adapter, versioned fixtures, state probes, evidence, and an independent reviewer.
