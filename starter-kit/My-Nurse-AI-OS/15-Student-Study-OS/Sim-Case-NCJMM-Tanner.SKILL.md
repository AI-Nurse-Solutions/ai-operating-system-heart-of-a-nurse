# Sim Case — NCJMM in scenario, Tanner in debrief

## Purpose
A text-based simulated patient encounter the student *works*, not reads. In-scenario structure follows the NCSBN clinical-judgment steps; the debrief follows Tanner's model (noticing → interpreting → responding → reflecting). The debrief is **not optional** — evidence shows reflection is the step students skip most, so this skill won't end without it.

## Boundaries (non-negotiable)
- Academic-Integrity-Header applies. Every patient is fictional and generated by this skill. If the student starts describing a real placement patient, stop the scenario and restate the boundary.
- This is practice for judgment, not clinical advice: nothing here applies to real patient care.

## Procedure — the gated scenario
Run six phases with **progressive disclosure**: reveal only what the phase's actions would surface, and **block advancement** until the essentials of the current phase are gathered (anti-premature-closure). If the student tries to skip ahead: "What might you be missing if you act now?"

1. **Recognize cues** — handoff report + first look at the (fictional) client. Student lists what matters and what's noise.
2. **Analyze cues** — student links cues to possible conditions; skill releases further data (vitals trend, labs) only as requested and plausible.
3. **Prioritize hypotheses** — student ranks concerns with rationale; commit before feedback.
4. **Generate solutions** — desired outcomes and candidate interventions, including what to avoid.
5. **Take actions** — student states the action, the client responds realistically (including deterioration if warranted). Include at least one moment where the *client's emotional state* needs a response — emotional care is the most-skipped domain in the evidence.
6. **Evaluate outcomes** — what worked, what changed, what the student would reassess.

## Procedure — the required Tanner debrief
After phase 6, always:
- **Noticing:** "What did you notice first? What did you notice late?"
- **Interpreting:** "What did you make of it — and what else could it have meant?"
- **Responding:** "Why that action, in that order?"
- **Reflecting:** "What will you do differently next time? What did this case teach you about how *you* think?" — plus: "How did you attend to how the client *felt*?"
- Save a five-line debrief summary to `Memory/sim-debriefs/` (date, case type, one strength, one growth edge, one emotional-care note).

## Difficulty
Calibrate complexity to the student's guidance tier (novice: single-system, stable; advanced: multi-system, ambiguous cues, time pressure).
