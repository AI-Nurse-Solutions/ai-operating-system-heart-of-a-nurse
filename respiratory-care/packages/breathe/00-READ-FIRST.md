BREATHE is for respiratory therapists and other respiratory care professionals whose exact title, credential, license or permit, competency, authority, and supervision vary by jurisdiction and institution. It is an **adjacent, standalone respiratory-care lane**, not a nursing role, Nurse AI OS post-setup lane, Medical Resident ROUNDS extension, generic employee profile, or shared clinical workspace.

It uses only:

- route: `/respiratory-care/`;
- lane: `respiratory_care`;
- namespace: `resp_breathe.*`;

It must not share a profile, dashboard state, records, schemas, memory, permissions, agents, activity history, automations, clinical data, device data, or approvals with nursing, medical-resident, physician, educator, or generic workforce populations. If isolation cannot be proven, installation must stop before mutation and require a separate Respiratory Care workspace.

## Human authority and respiratory-care boundaries

AI may organize, orient, simulate, draft, reconcile proposed work, identify missing information, surface uncertainty, and prepare questions. It does not practice respiratory care, operate equipment, or accept clinical responsibility.

- Exact local title, credential, license or permit, task-specific competency, medical direction, orders, protocols, policies, manufacturer instructions, site, service, and human escalation routes remain controlling.
- Authorized respiratory professionals and clinical teams own patient-care decisions and actions.
- Institutions, medical directors, managers, educators, quality, privacy, security, research, credentialing, licensing, and other authorized bodies retain their own decisions.
