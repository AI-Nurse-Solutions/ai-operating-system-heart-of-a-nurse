The 30-day sprint

  1. Week 1: Listen before building.
  2. Week 2: Build the minimum useful kit.
  3. Week 3: Test in public, gently.
  4. Week 4: Package the doorway and decide the next circle.

Validated means

  • 10 nurses say the problem is real.
  • 5 nurses request the kit.
  • 1 nurse pays, pre-orders, or commits to a workshop.
  • A leader or educator asks how to bring it to a group.

No ROI guarantees. No clinical efficacy claims. Adoption signals and learning evidence only.

Where the side gig leads

The NIN/NAIO Nurse Entrepreneur Program

Formal launch: September 15–17
Founding enrollment opens at launch. Kit builders get first invitation.

The starter kit is Phase 0 in miniature. The full program is a guided, phase-based journey that turns nurses and healthcare professionals into AI-native founders — people who design, govern, and scale AI-enabled ventures, rather than just use AI tools. It is model-agnostic by design, impact-first by conviction, and governed by EDENA from day one — not as compliance, but as your competitive advantage.

Develop the steward→ Learn AI orchestration→ Build your own AI OS→ Launch a governed venture→ Scale an organization that benefits people and the planet

Ten levels, each earned by finishing a phase and passing its EDENA gate — from Steward (identity and values) to Steward Leader (scaling organizations that benefit society).

The journey, phase by phase

Foundations · Phases 0–2
0 · Identity

Before building AI, build the founder: mission, personal constitution, values, and your EDENA profile.

1 · Opportunity

Ask "what human problem deserves solving?" — interviews, market maps, validated evidence.

2 · AI-Native Founder

Learn AI thinking, not coding: prompting, research, memory, and your first founder AI assistant.

Founder OS · Phases 3–5
3 · AI Chief of Staff

Build your own Hermes-powered chief of staff — dashboard, knowledge vault, decision and reflection systems. The unique core of the program.

4 · AI Workforce

Hire agents, not people yet: an AI Company of One with governed departments.

5 · Workflow Engineering

Every business becomes inputs → decisions → actions → outputs → learning, with human gates.

Venture · Phases 6–7
6 · Build Your MVP

From idea to working product — course, kit, consultancy, community, SaaS, or media.

7 · First Revenue

Pricing, offers, and real customers — solving real problems, gathering testimonials, iterating.

Scale & Stewardship · Phases 8–10
8 · Scale

When demand exceeds capacity: AI departments and an executive agent staff.

9 · Sovereignty

Choose your deployment — cloud, local, self-hosted, institutional — and own your stack.

10 · Scale Good

Some organizations maximize impact, not profit. Healthcare, education, climate, community.

You are never handed a tool and left alone: Florence-X coordinates, Hermes does the work and remembers, EDENA keeps it governed — all rooted in nursing credibility (NIN) and a learning community (FMN).

The EDENA gate, every phase: Is it ethical? Is it defensible? Is it explainable? Does it respect privacy? Does it preserve human accountability? Does it create long-term value? No phase is complete until the answer is yes.

Not every company should maximize profit. Some maximize impact. The program treats impact enterprises — healthcare, education, climate, community, humanitarian — as first-class ventures, not afterthoughts.
