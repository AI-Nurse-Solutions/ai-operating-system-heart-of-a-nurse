# EDENA No-PHI Guardrails for Nurse AI Side Gigs

Before building any side-gig artifact, pass it through this gate.

## Green uses — okay to explore

- Personal learning plans.
- Public education about AI literacy and nursing stewardship.
- No-PHI productivity templates.
- Study systems that teach reasoning, not shortcut it.
- Content calendars, reflection prompts, and workshop outlines.
- Publicly available policy or research summaries with citations.
- Community needs mapping without patient, employer, or confidential details.

## Yellow uses — human review before sharing

- Paid digital products for nurses.
- Workshops, webinars, and coaching offers.
- Governance templates for leaders.
- AI prompt packs that could be misunderstood as clinical guidance.
- Case-style teaching examples, even if fictional.
- Claims about time savings, outcomes, skill development, or readiness.

## Orange / Red uses — stop and escalate

- Anything involving PHI or identifiable patient stories.
- Patient-specific clinical decisions, diagnosis, treatment, medications, or triage.
- EHR screenshots, chart excerpts, room numbers, dates, names, MRNs, images, or voice recordings.
- Employer confidential information, internal policies, non-public workflows, or incident details.
- Claims of certification, credentialing, competency validation, clinical readiness, deployment approval, legal compliance, or procurement approval.
- Autonomous outreach, automated enrollment, automatic payment, or automatic publication without human review.

## Required footer for every offer

> No PHI. No patient-specific clinical decisions. No employer secrets. No certification claim. Human judgment first.

## The 3 AM bedside test

Ask: **Would a tired bedside nurse at 3 AM understand what this is, what it is not, and where human judgment remains in charge?**

If not, rewrite before sharing.
