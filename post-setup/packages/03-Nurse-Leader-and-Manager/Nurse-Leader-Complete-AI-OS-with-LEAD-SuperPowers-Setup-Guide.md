# What this Complete Edition does

The Complete Edition creates a governed digital leadership office that helps a nurse leader:

- regain a clear view of priorities, approvals, commitments, and unresolved decisions;
- turn meetings into verified decisions, accepted owners, due dates, and follow-through;
- prepare evidence-aware briefs without disguising unknowns as facts;
- coordinate quality, workforce, operations, projects, resources, readiness, and communication in one view;
- prepare transparent staff and executive communication without sending it automatically;
- clarify delegation while preserving authority, capacity, fairness, and accountability;
- understand approved aggregate signals without jumping to cause or blame;
- manage change with affected-group, workflow, burden, risk, dependency, and readiness views;
- protect staff dignity by prohibiting surveillance, hidden profiling, and unsupported inference;
- govern AI and digital ideas before live use;
- preserve source freshness, decisions, corrections, action history, and rollback receipts;
- protect the leader's presence, recovery, relationships, priorities, career, and longer-term contribution.

It does not guarantee improved patient outcomes, staffing adequacy, compliance, financial return, safety, culture, or reduced liability. Those require valid measurement and independent human verification.

# Before setup

Confirm:

- A private, authenticated workspace is owned by the nurse leader.
- The actual leadership scope is clear: unit, department, service, program, or organization.
- Synthetic Demonstration Mode is acceptable for installation and testing.
- No patient information or patient-linked narrative is in the workspace.
- No personnel, performance, disciplinary, accommodation, employee-health, grievance, labor, investigation, privileged quality, peer-review, incident, credential, secret, or raw organizational-system data is present.
- Connections, shared access, external actions, new memory categories, and background automation are off.

- The Markdown dashboard can be used if an enhanced interface fails.

# Troubleshooting

## Setup stopped at preflight

Read the blocked control and remediation list. Do not ask Hermes to bypass it. Correct the environment, then rerun the read-only preflight.

## Foundation already exists

Hermes should verify and bind to the healthy foundation. It must not replace the Command Center, records, permissions, sources, or unrelated work.

## A duplicate dashboard or record appears

Use Pause All. Restore the last safe checkpoint and run a read-only inventory. Preserve the foundation source of truth and remove only the unapproved duplicate after preview.

## A critical test fails

Activation remains **Blocked**. Correct the cause and rerun the failed test plus related tests. Never waive a privacy, people, authority, evidence, external-action, dashboard, or recovery gate for convenience.

## Sensitive information was entered

Pause without repeating it. Use available deletion, memory, and Activity History controls, and follow the authorized organizational incident route.

## The dashboard feels overwhelming

Return to Focused view, keep only the Core Four, and leave every optional power inactive.

## The enhanced interface fails

Continue with the canonical Markdown Command Center. No critical control may depend only on the enhanced interface.

## The leader cannot tell what changed

Ask Hermes for the Activation Report, progress receipts, Activity History, and before/after change manifest.
