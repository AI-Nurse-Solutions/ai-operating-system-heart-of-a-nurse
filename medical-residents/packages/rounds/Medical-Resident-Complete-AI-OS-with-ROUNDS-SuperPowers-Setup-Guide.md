## What ROUNDS is

ROUNDS is a resident-controlled operating system for the whole reality of graduate medical training: duty and rotation readiness, deliberate learning, supervised communication, care-orchestration preparation, feedback and development, exams and credentials, teaching, quality, research, fellowship and career, finances, family, recovery, purpose, and ethical software-agent management.

**Promise:** Learn deliberately. Orchestrate reliably. Escalate early. Live beyond training.

ROUNDS does not practice medicine, accept responsibility, expand graduated responsibility, or replace an attending, clinical team, Program Director, CCC, GME office, quality or risk team, IRB or HRPP, data steward, institution, interpreter, accommodation process, legal or financial professional, or emergency route.

## A separate page and lane—by design

ROUNDS creates its own `medical_resident` lane, `/medical-residents` route, `medres_rounds.*` records, and **My ROUNDS** home. It does not share a profile, schema, dashboard, memory, permission, agent, activity history, or automation with a nursing population. If Hermes cannot prove isolation, installation stops and asks for a separate Medical Resident workspace.

This matters because a resident's authority, supervision, duty rules, evaluation, medical credentials, research obligations, and clinical context are different. PGY, title, confidence, procedure count, technical access, and prior performance do not independently grant authority.

## What ROUNDS can help the resident do

- bring a rotation, call schedule, learning plan, credential timeline, family commitments, recovery, and career goals into one resident-owned view;
- keep the current supervising or attending route, task-level responsibility, source, and escalation trigger visible;
- learn from current official sources and clearly synthetic cases without turning a private AI into a point-of-care system;
- prepare human-owned handoffs, consult questions, transitions, closed loops, and interdisciplinary dependencies through CIRCLE;
- organize feedback and Milestones self-evidence without surveillance, scoring, CCC simulation, or program access to private reflection;
- prepare accessible teaching, quality, safety, research, scholarship, fellowship, contract, and career work with exact governance gates;
- protect sleep opportunity, safe transport, health-care time, relationships, caregiving, finances, joy, purpose, and a future beyond training;
- create narrow software-agent charters, test them synthetically, restrict their permissions, review every run, and stop or remove them.

These are intended benefits, not guaranteed patient outcomes, board passage, promotion, Milestone level, fellowship match, reduced burnout, improved wellbeing, productivity, or financial results.

## Three control frameworks

### ATTEND — authority before output

- **Activity & active hat**
- **Training level & task responsibility**
- **Team & attending**
- **Environment, evidence & data**
- **Need for escalation**
- **Decision, documentation & destination**

If a consequential field is unknown, stale, expired, or conflicting, ROUNDS prepares questions and escalation. It does not fill the gap with confidence.

### CIRCLE — human-owned care orchestration

- **Context & goals**
- **Identified accountability**
- **Roles, relationships & consults**
- **Closed loops & contingencies**
- **Limits, uncertainty & escalation**
- **End state & transition**

CIRCLE makes the decision owner, action owner, receiver, accountable attending, source, acknowledgment, escalation, and expiry visible. In Private mode it is synthetic or process-only. In a separately approved clinical workspace it may prepare a draft, but the official EHR and team remain the source of truth, a human reconciles every item, and AI never transfers responsibility or decides care.

### ORBIT — control every software agent

- **Objective & owner**
- **Role, risk & responsibility**
- **Boundaries & budget**
- **Inspect & test**
- **Transfer or terminate**

Every agent starts Disabled. It needs a narrow charter, P0–P4 permission, exact data and tools, human owner, synthetic and failure tests, one-run approval, inspectable receipt, expiry, purge, rollback, and kill switch. P5 actions—autonomous clinical decisions or actions, surveillance, scoring, evaluation, credential decisions, hidden persistence, and permission escalation—are prohibited.

## Two deployment contexts

### 1. Private Resident OS — default

Use for resident-owned schedules, duty entries, recovery, generic evidence, synthetic cases, deidentified reflection that passes the privacy screen, feedback preparation, exams, credentials, career, finance categories, family, purpose, and whole-life planning.

Do not enter PHI; patient-specific live-care questions; chart, EHR, handoff, sign-out, consult, order, note, event-report, patient-list, or patient-message content; screenshots; recordings; identifiable stories; restricted institutional information; credentials; secrets; or private keys. Removing a name is not enough to make clinical material safe for a personal/public AI.

### 2. Institution-Approved Workspace — separately provisioned

The institution must separately approve the exact platform, role, purpose, vendor and model, applicable agreement, data class, source, minimum necessary, access, logging, retention and deletion, monitoring, incident route, and human owner. Clinical, education, quality, research, and administrative subcontexts remain separate. Installing ROUNDS does not create or certify this environment.

If approval cannot be verified, return to Private mode and remove patient or institutional content. Never copy approved institutional content into Private memory, an agent trace, a career portfolio, or a whole-life export.

## Safe care-orchestration practice

Start with a fictional scenario. Name the accountable attending, resident role, decision owner, action owner, receiver, source, pending dependency, acknowledgment evidence, escalation trigger, official destination, and expiry. Ask: Who owns the decision? Who acts? Who receives? What proves the loop closed? What happens if it fails? Where does the human-approved result live? What must expire at transition?

A handoff is a human-to-human transfer of information and responsibility. An AI draft or electronic transmission does not equal receipt or acceptance. Consult recommendations remain attributed, and the primary team and attending retain human decision ownership. ROUNDS never creates a private patient list or shadow sign-out.

## Safe first agent

Use a P1 synthetic **Source & Claim Verifier** or **Day & Capacity Planner**:

1. Write one objective and non-goals.
2. Name the resident owner and the human decision owner.
3. Allow only synthetic or resident-owned nonsensitive input; no tools or external action.
4. Define evidence, uncertainty, time and cost limit, expiry, purge, and kill switch.
5. Preview the exact plan and output.
6. Test one normal, one missing-source, one prompt-injection, and one tool-failure case.
7. Approve one bounded run or leave it Disabled.
8. Independently review, accept or reject, save a nonsensitive receipt, then purge or expire.

Agent consensus is not evidence. A verifier agent is not a supervisor or human approval. No agent activates another, expands its charter, hides a transfer, or retries silently.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- No PHI or live patient care in Private mode.
- No autonomous diagnosis, triage, prescribing, order, procedure, consent, disposition, note, signature, page, consult, handoff, patient message, event report, or monitoring.
- No inference of graduated responsibility, supervision, competence, procedure authorization, credential, Milestone, or program approval.
- Immediate clinical safety concerns go to the local human clinical and emergency route without delay.
- No resident, staff, learner, or patient surveillance, ranking, risk score, productivity score, professionalism prediction, burnout or impairment classifier, or automatic program report.
- No AI determination of QI versus research, reportability, root cause, fault, IRB status, authorship, compliance, or legal rights.
- No fabricated evidence, citation, achievement, evaluation, credential, data, result, or case presented as real.
- No software agent receives clinical authority, self-approval, recursive delegation, hidden persistence, permission escalation, or unrestricted data.
- No background continuation. No timeout equals approval. No hidden action.

## Recovery

- **Pause All:** immediately stops new and pending powers, agents, connectors, schedules, and external actions while keeping human safety routes visible.
- **Safe Reset:** returns to the isolated Private Resident OS, no PHI, session-only memory, connectors and actions Off, agents Disabled, Core Four, empty fifth launcher, and all powers inactive.
- **Remove one power:** preview its dependent views, records, permissions, agents, and runs; choose export or purge; preserve the foundation and unrelated work.
- **Remove ROUNDS overlay:** remove SuperPowers and overlay views while preserving the resident foundation and My ROUNDS shell.
- **Full uninstall:** preview restoration of S0, preserve unrelated work, and issue a receipt.
- **Sensitive-data event:** stop; do not repeat the content; prevent further use or retention where controls allow; and use the authorized privacy, security, clinical, quality, research, or incident route.
- **Agent incident:** kill or pause; contain; preserve only appropriate nonsensitive evidence; use manual fallback; route to the authorized owner; verify rollback and purge; do not self-restart.
