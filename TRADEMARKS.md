# Protect the Name Without Closing the Platform

**File:** `TRADEMARKS.md`
**Applies to:** the Nurse AI OS name, logo, visual identity, and certification marks.
**Does not apply to:** the source code (Apache License 2.0) or the documentation (CC BY 4.0), which remain open under their respective licenses.

---

## 1. The Mark

Nurse AI OS™ is a trademark of Robert Domondon. An application to register the Nurse AI OS mark is pending.

Use the ™ symbol with the name in formal contexts. Do not use the ® symbol; the registered-trademark symbol applies only after federal registration is granted, and only for the goods and services the registration covers.

The software license and the trademark are separate instruments. Apache License 2.0 grants broad rights to use, modify, distribute, and commercially deploy the code. It expressly does not grant rights to the project's trade names, trademarks, or service marks (Apache 2.0, Section 6). Everything the code license permits, this policy leaves permitted. This policy governs only the identity.

## 2. Permitted Uses — No Permission Required

You may, without asking:

- Refer to the project by name to identify it factually: in documentation, articles, research, coursework, presentations, and conversation.
- State truthful relationships: "built with Nurse AI OS," "compatible with Nurse AI OS," "runs on Nurse AI OS," "a fork of Nurse AI OS."
- Critique, review, compare, parody, or report on the project, favorably or unfavorably. Journalism, research, and commentary that use the name to discuss the project are always permitted. This policy will never be asserted against good-faith criticism.
- Fork the code and say your project is derived from Nurse AI OS, provided your fork carries its own name.

## 3. Prohibited Uses

You may not:

- Call a fork, distribution, or hosted service the "official" Nurse AI OS, or name it in a way that causes material confusion with the official project.
- Use the Nurse AI OS logo or visual identity without written permission.
- State or imply endorsement, certification, partnership, or organizational affiliation that does not exist.
- Use a confusingly similar name for a competing distribution or hosted service.
- Issue certificates, badges, or credentials under the Nurse AI OS name or marks. Certification is a controlled program; see Section 4.

## 4. Certification and Badges

Official Nurse AI OS certifications and badges are issued only through the official certification program under a controlled trademark-license agreement. No other party may issue, sell, or display Nurse AI OS certifications or badges, or represent training as leading to official certification without a written agreement.

Custody of the certification marks and badge-issuance authority is designated to transfer to the project's nonprofit governance steward upon its formation, under the succession framework described in `GOVERNANCE.md`.

## 5. Enforcement Posture

The mark exists to make the project recognizable, not to make it coercive. Enforcement is defensive:

1. First response to misuse is correction — a factual notice, a reference to this policy, and a bounded remediation timeline.
2. Formal action is reserved for bad-faith imposture, material confusion that persists after correction, or misuse that endangers patients or the public.
3. This policy is never enforced against critique, comparison, journalism, research, or good-faith open-source work.

## 6. Ownership and Succession

Copyright and trademark are currently held by Robert Domondon as an individual. Upon formation of the designated successor entity, ownership will transfer by written intellectual-property assignment, and this file will be updated to reflect the new holder. The permissions and prohibitions in this policy survive that transfer unchanged.
