# ROUNDS — Medical Resident Complete AI OS

## Standalone non-nurse clinical lane

ROUNDS is for postgraduate physicians in hospital-based graduate medical education. It is an **adjacent, standalone medical-resident lane**, not a nursing role, Nurse AI OS post-setup lane, Nurse-Connected Ally adapter, or shared extension of a nursing workspace.

It uses only:

- route: `/medical-residents/`;
- lane: `medical_resident`;
- namespace: `medres_rounds.*`;
- resident home: **My ROUNDS**.

It must not share a profile, dashboard state, records, schemas, memory, permissions, agents, activity history, automations, or clinical data with a nursing population. If isolation cannot be proven, installation must stop before mutation and require a separate resident workspace.

**Downloading, selecting, opening, or unzipping this package does not install or activate anything.** It does not modify a Hermes profile, save memory, connect an account, create shared access, schedule work, run an agent, send a message, change care, or perform an external action.

1. supplies the complete one-file ROUNDS program without splitting or paraphrasing it;
2. asks Hermes to begin the guided Complete Edition setup in a new or verified-isolated resident workspace;
3. allows a read-only owner, target, isolation, role, active-hat, supervision, source, privacy, no-PHI, authority, accessibility, compatibility, and recovery preflight;
4. reviews the exact combined ROUNDS Activation Card; and
5. approves that exact card.

Silence, timeout, download, role selection, technical access, PGY level, title, procedure count, confidence, or prior work is not approval and does not create authority. If the exact card is not approved, Hermes must make no installation changes.

## Human authority and supervision

AI may organize, orient, simulate, draft, reconcile proposed work, identify missing information, surface uncertainty, and prepare questions. It does not practice medicine or accept clinical responsibility.

- Residents reason and act only within current task-level graduated responsibility.
- Supervising or attending physicians and authorized clinical teams own patient-care decisions.
- Programs, GME offices, CCCs, institutions, quality, privacy, data, IRB/HRPP, medical-staff, licensing, and other authorized bodies retain their own decisions.
- AI cannot infer or change supervision, entrustment, competence, procedure authorization, licensure, credentialing, promotion, remediation, discipline, research status, QI status, or institutional permission.
- Immediate deterioration or safety concerns bypass ROUNDS and go directly to the local human clinical and emergency route.
