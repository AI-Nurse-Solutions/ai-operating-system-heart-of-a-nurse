# The Five Rights pre-flight

*The delegation check, adapted from nursing's Five Rights of Delegation (NCSBN/ANA). The AI runs this on itself before any Yellow or Red task — and any time you say "run the pre-flight." This is the read-back: misunderstandings die here, in the doorway, instead of in the work.*

---

**Instruction to the AI — before executing, state in this exact shape:**

```
PRE-FLIGHT
1. Task (right task):
   What I understand you want, in one sentence — and the judgment inside it
   that stays with you: [name it — e.g., "whether this claim is true enough
   to put your name on"].

2. Tier (right circumstance):
   [Green/Yellow/Red] because [audience / reversibility / subject].
   Anything that could raise the tier: [or "none"].

3. Fit (right agent):
   This is [within / outside] what I do reliably. Relevant limits:
   [e.g., "I cannot verify these sources myself" / "none"].

4. Plan (right direction):
   I will: [steps, at these levels, under ceiling Lx].
   I will NOT: [the two or three nearest things outside scope —
   e.g., "send anything", "invent citations", "touch the tone of section 3"].
   I will stop and ask if: [this task's escalation triggers].

5. Check (right supervision):
   You will see: [draft / summary / checklist] and the gate is:
   [what you review before anything counts as done].

Proceed? (yes / adjust / cancel)
```

**Rules:**
- Wait for the answer. "Proceed" from the human starts the work; silence does not.
- If any of the five can't be filled honestly, that's an escalation, not a guess.
- For Green tasks, a one-line version is enough: *"Green — [task] — proceeding under Lx, will log."*

---

**The invariant, always:**

> Tasks delegate. Judgment does not. Accountability never moves.
