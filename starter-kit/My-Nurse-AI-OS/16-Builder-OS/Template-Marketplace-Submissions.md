# Sharing What You Build — the Template & Skill Marketplace (submissions)

Built a skill or template that works? Sharing it is the community economy of Nurse AI OS — and the Steward Council's review is what makes the shared shelf trustworthy. This page is the whole process.

## What can be submitted

Skills (SKILL.md), templates, prompt packs, and workflow files that are: **no-PHI by construction**, within nursing scope, honestly described, and useful to at least one of the four audiences (students/educators, bedside/APRN, leaders, entrepreneurs).

## Before you submit — the format bar

1. Use the **Governed-Skill-Template** (`05-Templates/Governed-Skill-Template.md`): EDENA frontmatter (tier, gate, owner, review date) is required; Yellow+ items include the fail-closed self-audit footer.
2. Load the relevant shared header (Academic-Integrity for study skills, Builder-Boundary for business skills) rather than restating boundaries loosely.
3. Run your own **Boundary-Counsel** review first and note the verdict in your submission.
4. Name a real author (you) and a version. Anonymous or unversioned items aren't reviewable.

## How to submit

Email the file to **great.ai.nurses@gmail.com** with subject `MARKETPLACE SUBMISSION: <name>`, or open a pull request against the site repository. Include: what it does in two sentences, which audience, tier, and your Boundary-Counsel verdict.

## What happens next (per the Steward Council charter)

- Council members review against the **Skill & MCP Vetting Checklist** — named author, read-before-approve, least privilege, correct tier, boundaries present.
- **Two approvals** → published as `community-reviewed`. **Unanimous** → `steward-core`. Any single member can quarantine on a safety concern — investigation follows, not argument.
- The decision (verdict, rationale, votes) is recorded in the public ledger. Declines come with reasons and, where possible, a path to fix.
- Everything published gets re-reviewed on the 90-day cycle; stale items are archived, not silently kept.

## What sharing is not

Publication is **not** endorsement, certification, clinical validation, or a compliance claim — the shelf label says a named nurse read it and it met the bar, nothing more. Your name stays on your work; improvements by others fork with attribution.

> Agents propose. Humans judge. Nurses steward.
