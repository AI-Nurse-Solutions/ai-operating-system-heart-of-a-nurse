Step 0 — the readiness check (2 minutes)

You check your equipment at the start of every shift. Same here — two minutes now saves an hour of frustration later.

You'll need a desktop or laptop — Mac or Windows; not a phone, tablet, or Chromebook. Why it's worth it: on your own computer, your files and your AI's memory stay local — on your machine, not in someone's cloud. Rough guide: a computer from the last ~5 years with at least 8 GB of memory and a few GB free usually does fine — the official Hermes site lists current requirements.

Prep the field — like clearing a sterile field before a procedure: your system will live in one dedicated folder (the Starter Kit builds it), and nothing private should live inside it — no tax documents, no family photos, no work files. On a shared family computer, consider a separate user account: your own med cart instead of a shared one.

If Hermes won't install, it's your computer's specs — not you, and not a mistake you made. A cloud option (the VPS path) is coming so any device can run a full system.

No qualifying computer yet? You're still in — today. Take the free AI course and the 55 free AI mentors (they run in any browser), come to the monthly Lamp Huddle, join the community list, and take the SOUL Quiz now — your SOUL file will be ready the day your system is. Your participation and your voice matter to us now, not after you buy hardware. AI is coming to healthcare with or without nursing at the table — the nurses preparing today will lead that change instead of being led by it. You are the future Florence Nightingales, the ones who will decide how care intelligence is born.

The cheat sheet, right here:

Install & first launch
Download from the official site for your computer (Mac or Windows) and follow their steps. Open it. If it asks about an AI model or provider, the default is fine — you can change it later. Menus move as Hermes updates; when in doubt, their official docs are the truth.
Your first conversation
Type like you'd text a colleague. Start with: "I'm a nurse setting up my Nurse AI OS. I'll give you my SOUL file next — treat it as my standing instructions."
Permissions — give it a room, not the house
When Hermes asks for permission to access files, grant access only to your My-Nurse-AI-OS folder — the one from the Starter Kit — and decline anything broader (full disk, Desktop, Downloads, Photos). Think of a float nurse's badge: access to the assigned unit, not every floor of the hospital. Granted too much by accident? Remove it in your computer's privacy settings.
Smart habit — a fresh Gmail for your AI life
Just like you don't do personal banking from the unit's charting station, create a free, separate Gmail (something like yourname.ai@gmail.com) used only for your AI work, courses, and community. Your personal and family email stays private — and the new account's free Google Drive and Docs pair beautifully with your system later.
The five phrases that do most of the work
"Run my morning huddle." · "Give me a Kardex entry for this session." · "Make that correction permanent." · "Before you start, interview me like an admission assessment." · "Show me your plan before you do anything." — that last one keeps your hand on every decision.

Rather not install anything yet? You can do steps 2–5 in the browser AI you already use (ChatGPT or Claude) — paste instead of upload. Hermes just makes it permanent.

Take the SOUL Quiz, then give Hermes your SOUL file

This is my SOUL file. Treat it as my standing instructions for how to support me. Confirm what you understood about my values and my boundaries — especially what I will never delegate.

Build your dashboard — one prompt — and bookmark it

Your starter kit includes a private, browser-only dashboard. This prompt has Hermes personalize it from your SOUL Quiz answers — your name, your priorities, your first goals — automatically:

Using my SOUL file, personalize the Local HTML Life Dashboard in my starter kit (08-Integrations/Local-HTML-Life-Dashboard). Fill in my name, my top priorities in each sphere, and my first goals and habits from my quiz answers. Save it as My-Dashboard.html in my Nurse AI OS folder and tell me exactly where it is.

Open the file it creates and bookmark it. That's your station board — everything you're building, one glance, nothing uploaded anywhere.

Later, if you ever want more: a Notion version, messaging access, and sovereign/on-premise setups exist in advanced topics. Not now. The bookmark is enough.

Run your first prompts

You're set up. Now make it earn its keep — pick whichever fits tonight:

The daily rhythm (start tomorrow morning)

Run my morning huddle. Ask me one question: "What is your number one priority today?" Then suggest three specific ways you can help — drafts, research, summaries, planning — never clinical decisions or anything with patient data. Ask which to start.

Students — one lecture, tamed

Here are my lecture notes on [topic]. Build me a plain-language study map, then quiz me Socratically — five questions, one at a time. Don't give me the answer until I've reasoned through it.

Staff — get one thing off your plate

Build me a shift-recovery checklist based on my SOUL file — what helps me decompress, in order, for the first hour after I get home.

Leaders — your first governance move

Help me map one workflow on my unit (no patient data — just the process): what burden AI could reduce, what harm it could introduce, and where a human must stay in the loop.

When these feel natural, the six workflows take you the rest of the way — one per week, until the system truly knows you.

The rules that protect everyone

Plain-language safety rules

You memorized the rights of medication administration. These are shorter, and they protect your patients, your license, and you.

No patient data. Ever.

No names, charts, screenshots, room numbers, record numbers, dates, or stories that could identify a patient — in any AI tool, at any level. If you're unsure whether something counts: it counts. Leave it out.

AI drafts. You decide.

Every AI output is a draft until a human reviews it. Nothing gets sent, published, or acted on without your judgment.

Your license is not outsourceable.

No AI here supports clinical decisions, assessments, or patient-specific care questions. It carries your busywork — never your judgment.

Employer systems stay at work.

No EHR screens, no confidential policies, no identifiable staff or student data. Public, fictional, or personal material only — unless your organization's compliance team has explicitly approved a tool and workflow.

When in doubt, ask a human.

Bring gray areas to the Lamp Huddle or email great.ai.nurses@gmail.com. A question asked early beats an incident reported late.

These rules have a name: EDENA — Ethical Design & Enablement for Nursing Augmentation — the governance framework in every layer of Nurse AI OS. Every action gets classified before it runs: 🟢 Green — may proceed 🟡 Yellow — you approve first 🔴 Red — human authorization mandatory You'll meet it formally in the workbook — for now, the five rules above are all you need. When you're ready to make your AI follow them automatically, download the Governance Kit — the rules as files your system loads every session.
