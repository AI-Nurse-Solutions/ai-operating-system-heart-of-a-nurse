The SOUL Quiz Companion Guide

Every question in the SOUL Quiz can be read more than one way — and that's by design, because a student, a staff nurse, a manager, an educator, and the person who simply loves a nurse are all welcome here. This guide shows the span of good answers for each role, marks what you can safely skip for later, tells you exactly which button to press at the end, and walks you through handing the result to Hermes.

Download the Companion Guide (PDF) → Open the SOUL Quiz

The quiz lives entirely in your browser — nothing you answer is ever sent to us. This guide is just the interpreter beside you.

Before you start

Two truths that take the pressure off

Complete answers are worth the extra minutes

Your SOUL file is Hermes' admission assessment of you. The richer the intake, the better the care plan from day one — a full identity sentence and honest values save you weeks of correcting a stranger. If a question makes you pause, that pause usually means the answer matters.

And nothing here is carved in stone

Hermes keeps getting to know you as your interactions deepen — every "make that correction permanent" adds to what the quiz began. Skip what you're unsure of, come back, retake, re-download. The quiz is a first conversation, not a final exam.

The one rule of thumb for every question: answer for the season you're in, not the title on your badge. A manager three months from retirement and a manager three months into the role should give very different answers — and both are right.

Stage 1 · Who are you?

The intake — four quick fields

"Name you want Hermes to use" Required

Whatever you want to hear back. First name, nickname, even "Nurse M." It shapes tone, nothing else.

"Which best describes you right now?" Required

Pick the closest — right now, not your whole history. A staff nurse in grad school is still "Staff nurse" if the bedside is where this season lives. Educators: choose "Nurse leader / manager / educator." And if you're not a healthcare professional at all — the spouse, parent, or friend of a nurse setting up your own assistant — choose "Other" with pride. This system was built for a nurse's whole household.

"Which spheres do you want Hermes to support?" Required · at least one
StudentPersonal + Professional. School lives in Professional — that's where your study support comes from.
Staff nursePersonal + Professional to start; add Side Gig or Interest when the basics feel steady.
Leader / ManagerPersonal + Professional; add Community if you mentor, precept, or serve outside the walls.
EducatorPersonal + Professional; Community is a natural third for teaching and advocacy beyond your school.
Not a clinicianPersonal is your anchor; add Side Gig or Interest freely. Professional works fine for any career — it isn't nurses-only.

Two or three spheres is the sweet spot for a first pass. You can retake the quiz any time to add more.

"How much time can you give this quiz today?"

Be honest — choosing "15 min" is not failing, it's triage. Multi-session is respectable; your answers stay in your browser while you work.

Stage 2 · Core SOUL

The heart of the file — worth your best ten minutes

This stage becomes Core-SOUL.md, the base layer Hermes always loads. Four things here are required; everything else can grow later.

"In one sentence, who are you and what are you about right now?" Required

The most misread question in the quiz — it's asking about this season, not your resume. Good answers across the span:

Student"A second-year nursing student trying to pass pharm, keep my job, and still be a person."
Staff nurse"An ICU nurse of eight years chasing my CCRN while rebuilding my energy after nights."
Leader / Manager"A new unit manager learning to lead former peers without losing my family's evenings."
Educator"A clinical instructor redesigning my courses for the AI era without giving up my weekends."
Not a clinician"A nurse's husband running our household logistics and slowly building my woodworking business."
"3–5 non-negotiable values" Required

Not what sounds noble — what you actually refuse to trade. If "rest" is a value you keep breaking, listing it tells Hermes to protect it. Examples: dignity, honesty, evidence, family, rest · fairness, loyalty, growth, faith, humor. Single words are perfect.

"One thing Hermes should always remember about you"
Student"I learn best by explaining things out loud."
Staff nurse"I work nights — never suggest morning routines."
Leader / Manager"Nothing gets scheduled before school drop-off."
Educator"I say yes too often; help me notice."
Not a clinician"I'm the primary caregiver for my aging mother."
Voice & communication — length, formality, pushback, words to avoid

Answer length and formality: how you like to receive information, not how you write. Pushback style is the important one: "Always push back when I am wrong" gives you a colleague; "Push back when stakes are high" gives you a diplomat; "Stay agreeable" gives you a mirror — choose the first or second unless you have a real reason. Words to avoid: "no toxic positivity," "no corporate-speak," "never call me champ" — all legitimate entries.

The two boundary checkboxes Required — the stewardship gate

The quiz will not continue until you check both: no PHI, and Hermes does not make clinical decisions. If you're not a clinician, check them anyway and read them as: no private data about other people's health, and the AI advises — humans decide. This is the one non-negotiable in the whole quiz, for everyone.

"People, employers, or projects Hermes must never write about externally" Can grow later

Employer names, family members' names, clients of your side project. Blank is acceptable today; add names the first time Hermes drafts something public.

"Wellbeing rule when I'm tired, overwhelmed, or unsafe"

What you want Hermes to do when it notices you're running on empty: slow down, suggest rest, refer you to human support, or pause work entirely. Nurses are famously bad at answering this one honestly — answer as the nurse you'd want caring for you.

"Decisions I always make myself" / "Work Hermes may draft without asking"

The delegation split — your own five rights of delegation, applied to yourself:

StudentMine: anything graded, money, health. Draftable: study guides, flashcards, schedules.
Staff nurseMine: clinical, license-related, family, finances. Draftable: prep checklists, summaries, emails.
Leader / ManagerMine: personnel, budget, evaluations. Draftable: agendas, drafts of communications, project plans.
EducatorMine: grades and academic judgments. Draftable: rubric drafts, case scenarios (fictional), lesson outlines.
Not a clinicianMine: money, parenting, health, legal. Draftable: meal plans, itineraries, business drafts.

Stage 3 · Sphere passes

Short passes per sphere — every one of these can wait All skippable

Nothing in Stage 3 blocks you. Leave any field blank and keep moving — a thin sphere file today beats an abandoned quiz. Here are the questions people most often read two ways:

Personal · "What does a good week look like outside work/school?"

Describe the week you want to protect, not your current survival pattern. "Two real dinners with my kids, one run, church on Sunday, in bed by eleven twice" — that specificity is what lets Hermes defend it.

Personal · "How should Hermes show up here?" (Coach / Planner / Journal partner / Quiet helper)

Coach nudges you; Planner organizes you; Journal partner reflects with you; Quiet helper waits to be asked. When in doubt, Quiet helper — you can promote it later.

Professional · "Where are you in your career right now?"

Students: "School." Educators: usually "Experienced" or "Leadership" — pick where your influence sits. Not a clinician? This sphere still works for your own career; choose whatever stage fits, or "Transition."

Professional · "What CANNOT enter Hermes from work?"
StudentEverything from clinical placements — the no-PHI rule covers clinicals exactly as it covers employment.
Staff nursePHI, internal policies, incident details, anything from the EHR.
Leader / ManagerAll of the above plus staffing data, personnel issues, anything HR-adjacent.
EducatorStudent records and grades — FERPA is your PHI.
Not a clinicianYour own employer's confidential data — same principle, different building.
Community · "Voice you want here" (Pastoral / Professional / Prophetic / Peer / Organizer)

Pastoral comforts, Professional informs, Prophetic challenges, Peer walks alongside, Organizer mobilizes. Pick the voice your community needs from you — not the one that feels safest.

Side Gig · "Your stewardship boundary"

The line your business will never cross even when it would sell: "no exploitative copy, no medical claims, no clinical authority I don't hold." Non-clinicians too — every honest business has one.

Interests · "The anti-goal"

The most quietly important question in the quiz: what you do not want this hobby to become. "Don't let baking become a business." "Don't optimize the joy out of my garden." It tells Hermes when to stop helping.

Stage 5 · EDENA tiers  &  Stage 6 · The buttons

The finish line — tiers, downloads, and exactly which button to press

Stage 5 · Tier assignment (Green / Yellow per sphere)

This is EDENA — the conscience layer — asking how much rope each sphere gets. Green: low-risk help may proceed. Yellow: Hermes drafts, you approve first. Onboarding keeps everyone in Green and Yellow — higher tiers are earned later, deliberately. The defaults are well-chosen; accepting them is a complete answer.

Stage 6 · You'll see many buttons. Here's the map.

Press this one: "Download all (bundle .md)"

One click, every SOUL file — Core-SOUL.md plus one file per sphere. This is the button. Everything else on the screen is optional.

Safely ignore: "Export OS Config (naio-soul.json)"

A machine-readable config for the advanced NAIO installer. If you don't know you need it, you don't need it yet.

Optional fun: the tier-map card + "Share on LinkedIn"

A shareable image generated entirely in your browser. Download the card first, then attach it to the pre-filled post — LinkedIn won't attach it for you.

Then: "Back to Start Here — step 3 →"

That's your route onward — the Starter Kit and the handoff to Hermes, below.

Uploading to Hermes — the handoff

Put the files where they live

Unzip your Starter Kit if you haven't (Documents is perfect). Move your downloaded SOUL files into My-Nurse-AI-OS/01-SOUL/. One governed home, everything findable.

Hand Hermes the Core file

Drag Core-SOUL.md into the Hermes chat (or paste its contents), then say:

This is my SOUL file. Treat it as my standing instructions for how to support me. Confirm what you understood about my values and boundaries.

Read the summary back — and correct it

Hermes will tell you what it understood. Fix anything wrong, then say "make that correction permanent." This is the deepening: the quiz starts the relationship, the corrections grow it.

Skipped things? Perfect — that's a feature. Everything you left blank in Stage 3, every name you didn't list, every sphere you didn't choose: add them in conversation whenever they come up, or retake the quiz and re-download. A SOUL file is a living chart, not a stone tablet.
