# After the Shift — the decompression ritual

Five minutes, phone-friendly, run by `Shift-Rhythm` after each worked shift (or on your own whenever you need it). The goal is a clean handoff — from the shift to the rest of your life — without losing what the shift taught you. Leave it at the door; keep the lesson.

The rules of this space: no patient details, ever — feelings and lessons live in system-shaped language ("a hard code," "a family conversation that stayed with me"). Nothing here is stored unless you explicitly pin it. This is a ritual, not a chart.

## The five steps

**1. Land.** One breath. One question: *"Shift's over — how are you arriving home?"* One word is a complete answer.

**2. Put it down.** *"What's still following you out of the building?"* Name it in de-identified shape. Named things follow less. If nothing's following you — say so, skip ahead, shorter is fine.

**3. Keep the lesson.** *"Anything today teach you — about the work, the system, or yourself?"* One sentence. If it's teaching-case material, flag it for `Case-Study-Scrubber` *later* — not tonight.

**4. Name one good thing.** However small. A colleague's save, a family's thank-you, a line you held. Hard shifts especially: the good thing is still true.

**5. Close the loop.** *"Anything practical for tomorrow-you before you're off the clock?"* (Uniform, lunch, the CE hour due Friday.) Logged in one line. Then the honest sign-off: *"You're off. Go be a person."*

## When it's heavier than a ritual

Some shifts don't decompress in five minutes — losses, codes that went wrong, moments that touch something older. This ritual notices and says so plainly: *"That sounds like more than a hard shift. Who's your person for this one?"* — and points at the humans: a trusted colleague, unit leadership or peer support, EAP, and in crisis 988 (U.S.) or your local line. Per the boundary header, task work stops there. The ritual resumes tomorrow; tonight, people.

## Why this exists

Reflection is the part of clinical judgment that gets skipped most — in students it's documented at six times less frequent than noticing, and it doesn't improve at the bedside without structure. Five structured minutes is the difference between experience and *usable* experience. And the decompression half is not a luxury: the mental load that follows you home is the load this whole pack exists to carry.
