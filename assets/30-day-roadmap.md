# The 30-Day Nurse AI OS Roadmap

> *Carry the lamp. Keep the ledger. Build the system in 30 days.*

A free, step-by-step plan to build your nurse-centered AI Operating System — one calm habit at a time. Three tracks (Student / Staff Nurse / Leader) sharing one architecture.

**Core promise:** In 30 days you will have a working AI OS that supports you across all three spheres — **Personal, Professional, and Community/Entrepreneurial** — with safety, dignity, and human agency at the center.

**Core rule:**
> Agents propose. Humans judge. Nurses steward.

**Boundary:** No PHI. No patient-specific clinical decisions. No replacement for licensed judgment.

---

## How to use this roadmap

- **Time budget:** ~20 minutes/day, or one 2-hour Sunday block per week.
- **Pick your track:** Student, Staff Nurse, or Leader. The architecture is shared; the use cases differ.
- **EDENA tier** (Green/Yellow/Orange/Red) is shown on each task. Stay in Green and Yellow this month. Save Orange and Red for after the course.
- **Weekly Lamp Huddle:** Join the free live community call to ask questions and share wins.

## Week 1 — Foundation: Values, Boundaries, Workspace

> *"Before the tool, the steward."*

**Goal:** Set up your AI workspace, write your values, and lock the no-PHI boundary.

### Day 1 — Write your AI values (Green)
- Open a blank doc. Answer: *Why am I using AI? What is it for? What is it NOT for?*
- Save as `MY-AI-VALUES.md`.

### Day 2 — Set the no-PHI boundary (Green)
- Download the [No-PHI Boundary Checklist](https://nurse-ai-os.org/) from the starter kit.
- Identify which accounts/devices are PHI-free and which are not.

### Day 3 — Choose your AI host (Green)
- Pick one: ChatGPT, Claude, Hermes on MacBook, or Hermes on a VPS.
- Confirm it's a non-personal account (recommended) and not tied to clinical data.

### Day 4 — Install Obsidian (Green)
- Download Obsidian. Create a vault: `My-Nurse-AI-OS`.
- Build the folder structure from the starter kit (`00-Start-Here`, `01-SOUL`, `02-Skills`, `03-Memory`, `04-Projects`, `05-Learning`).

### Day 5 — Write your first SOUL.md (Green)
- One page: who you are, what you value, how you prefer answers, what you will NOT delegate.
- Save to `01-SOUL/Personal-SOUL.md`.

### Day 6 — Three first prompts (Green)
- Try one prompt in each sphere: Personal, Professional, Community.
- Save each to `05-Learning/first-prompts.md`.

### Day 7 — Weekly review (Green)
- 15 minutes. What worked? What felt unsafe? What do you want next week?
- Save to `03-Memory/weekly-reviews/W1.md`.

**Week 1 checkpoint:** You have a workspace, a values file, a boundary, and a habit of weekly review.

## Week 2 — Personal Sphere: Reclaim Time and Attention

> *"The lamp first lights the one who carries it."*

**Goal:** Use AI to reclaim time, energy, and presence in your own life.

### Track-specific focus

| Track | Week 2 emphasis |
|---|---|
| **Student** | Study planning, energy tracking, deadline triage, burnout early warning |
| **Staff Nurse** | Off-shift recovery, meal/family planning, finances, sleep & energy |
| **Leader** | Calendar triage, executive function, decision journal, boundaries |

### Days 8–9 — Personal planning (Green)
- Ask AI to help draft a weekly plan based on your real obligations.
- Save the prompt pattern to `02-Skills/personal/weekly-planning.md`.

### Days 10–11 — Knowledge inbox (Green)
- Create `05-Learning/knowledge-inbox.md`.
- Drop links, screenshots-of-text, and ideas in for 2 days. Ask AI to summarize at the end.

### Days 12–13 — Wellbeing prompt (Green)
- Build a daily reflection prompt: mood, energy, gratitude, friction.
- Save to `02-Skills/personal/daily-reflection.md`.

### Day 14 — Weekly review (Green)
- What time did you reclaim? Where did AI overreach? Adjust your SOUL.md.

**Week 2 checkpoint:** You have a personal AI rhythm that gives back at least 2 hours/week.

## Week 3 — Professional Sphere: Reduce Burden, Protect License

> *"Use the counting mind to protect the caring heart."*

**Goal:** Apply AI to your professional work — without PHI, without replacing judgment.

### Track-specific focus

| Track | Week 3 emphasis |
|---|---|
| **Student** | Clinical reasoning practice, NCLEX Socratic tutor, research appraisal |
| **Staff Nurse** | Template-only charting prep, policy navigation, handoff prep, certification roadmap |
| **Leader** | Meeting prep, policy brief drafting, AI vendor evaluation, EDENA tier assignment |

### Day 15 — Install the Obsidian + Hermes Second-Brain skill (Yellow)
- Download from the [starter kit](https://nurse-ai-os.org/).
- Set up sync between your devices.

### Day 16 — Clinical reasoning, not clinical answers (Green)
- Build a Socratic tutor prompt: *"Ask me questions about this topic. Do not give me the answer until I've reasoned through it."*
- Save to `02-Skills/professional/socratic-tutor.md`.

### Day 17 — Research appraisal (Green)
- Use the EDENA-AS evidence rubric: source, methodology, bias, applicability.
- Ask AI to summarize a paper using the rubric. Always verify the citation yourself.

### Day 18 — Meeting / handoff prep (Yellow)
- Use only non-PHI inputs: agendas, topics, your own notes.
- Build a reusable prep skill in `02-Skills/professional/meeting-prep.md`.

### Day 19 — Policy / vendor brief (Yellow — Leaders) | Care plan template (Green — Students/Staff)
- Leaders: draft a one-page brief on an AI tool using the EDENA tier framework.
- Students/Staff: build a non-PHI care-plan template AI can fill from your notes.

### Day 20 — Visit the [NAIO Agent Registry](https://nurse-ai-os.org/) (Green)
- Pick one Green-tier agent. Review its claim vs. proof. Decide if you'd use it.

### Day 21 — Weekly review (Green)
- What admin time did you save? What did you almost over-trust? Update your SOUL.md.

**Week 3 checkpoint:** You have a professional AI workflow that respects your license and saves real hours.

## Week 4 — Community / Entrepreneurial Sphere + Stewardship

> *"The lamp is meant to light others."*

**Goal:** Use AI to amplify your voice, your community, and your stewardship of others.

### Track-specific focus

| Track | Week 4 emphasis |
|---|---|
| **Student** | LinkedIn profile, portfolio start, content from clinicals (de-identified) |
| **Staff Nurse** | Side projects, per-diem proposals, community advocacy, content engine |
| **Leader** | Thought leadership, grant/proposal drafting, council charter, mentor matching |

### Day 22 — Choose one community project (Green)
- Pick one: a LinkedIn post series, a community event, a unit pilot, a grant application.
- Create `04-Projects/<project-name>/`.

### Day 23 — Voice + audience prompt (Green)
- Define your audience, voice, and non-negotiables.
- Save to `02-Skills/community/voice.md`.

### Day 24 — Content / proposal draft (Yellow)
- Ask AI to draft the first version. You edit, you decide, you publish.

### Day 25 — EDENA tier check (Yellow)
- For every agent or workflow you've added this month, assign a tier.
- Save your tier map to `00-Start-Here/MY-EDENA-TIERS.md`.

### Day 26 — Audit and prune (Green)
- Which skills did you actually use? Archive the rest to `99-Archive/`.

### Day 27 — Backup + recovery test (Yellow)
- Confirm Obsidian Sync (or Syncthing) works. Restore one file from history. You should be able to recover.

### Day 28 — Share one win (Green)
- Post it. Tell a colleague. Bring it to the Lamp Huddle.

### Day 29 — Write your 60–90 day plan (Green)
- Based on what worked, set the next 60–90 days. Where do you want your AI OS in 3 months?

### Day 30 — Steward someone else (Green)
- Teach one nurse one thing you learned. Stewardship is the final exam.

**Week 4 checkpoint:** You have a working Nurse AI OS, a community presence, a tier-mapped governance layer, and a habit of teaching others.
