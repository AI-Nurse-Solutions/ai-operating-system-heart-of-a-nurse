Cost & access

Is this really free? What's the catch?
During the founding year, the core experience — the quizzes, workbook, starter kit, 30-Day Roadmap, and monthly Lamp Huddle — is free for nurses and nursing students. No credit card, no auto-enrollment, no trial that flips into charges. Later, guided cohorts, reviewed credentials, and institutional programs may carry a subscription. The core learning stays free for nurses. The "catch," honestly stated: we want founding nurses shaping how AI enters healthcare, and your feedback is worth more than tuition.
Do I need to be technical to start?
No. The whole setup is five steps on one page — install Hermes (with a plain-language cheat sheet beside you), take the SOUL Quiz, add your starter kit, build your dashboard, run your first prompts. One evening. If you'd rather not install anything yet, every step also works in the browser AI you already use. Either way, you cannot break anything.
What kind of computer do I need?
For the full system (steps 1–5): a Mac or Windows desktop or laptop — not a phone, tablet, or Chromebook — roughly from the last five years with 8 GB of memory or more (the official Hermes site has current requirements). Running it on your own computer means your files and your AI's memory stay local — yours, not in someone's cloud. If your computer doesn't qualify, that's the machine, not you: a cloud option (VPS) is coming, and meanwhile everything else works in any browser today — the free course and 55 AI mentors, the SOUL Quiz, the monthly Lamp Huddle, and the community.
I'm exhausted after shifts. How much time does this take?
About 20 minutes a day, and the roadmap is built so you can skip days without falling behind. Day 1 is designed to give you a real win — something that saves you time this week, not someday.

Safety & professional boundaries

Will this ever ask for patient information?
No — and we refuse it. No patient names, charts, screenshots, room numbers, record numbers, or stories that could identify a patient, at any level of the program. The system is designed to say no before it says yes. See the plain-language safety rules.
Could using AI put my license at risk?
The program is built around the opposite: everything here is no-PHI, non-clinical, and advisory. AI supports your learning, organization, and admin — never assessments, clinical decisions, or patient-specific questions. Your judgment and accountability stay with you, and every workflow includes a human-review gate. When something is a gray area, the rule is simple: leave it out and ask a human.
Is this a clinical tool or medical device?
No. Nurse AI OS is an education and personal-productivity program. It is not clinical decision support, not certified, not a deployment-ready clinical system, and it never claims to be. Anything institutional or clinical requires formal governance, security review, and organizational approval — which we say plainly, everywhere it applies.
My hospital hasn't approved any AI tools. Can I still do this?
Yes — because everything starts in your personal life and learning: your study plans, your career, your time, your projects. Nothing touches employer systems, EHRs, or workplace data. If you later want AI in your actual workplace, that's a governance conversation with your organization — and the leader pathway teaches you exactly how to have it.

What the words mean

What is Nurse AI OS?
Your personal AI "operating system" — a governed set of files, prompts, boundaries, and routines shaped around your values and your role. Not software you buy; a system you build, guided by us, that runs on AI tools you already have.
What is a SOUL file?
Your values-and-boundaries file: how you want AI to support you across personal, professional, and community life. The SOUL Quiz drafts it for you in about 2 minutes. It never contains patient details.
What is Hermes, and do I need it?
Hermes Agent is the free, open-source AI engine (from Nous Research) that your Nurse AI OS runs on — it can use tools, files, and automations, so your system remembers you between sessions. Installing it is step 1 of the five-step setup, and the cheat sheet covers everything you need for basic use. Not ready to install? Every step also works in a browser AI like ChatGPT or Claude — paste instead of upload — and you can move to Hermes whenever you like.
What do NAIO, EDENA, and "human gate" mean?
NAIO (Nurse AI Operating Intelligence) is the nurse-led stewardship layer behind the program. EDENA (Ethical Design & Enablement for Nursing Augmentation) is the governance framework: AI augments the nurse's judgment, never replaces it. A human gate is a checkpoint where a person must review and approve before AI output is used, sent, or acted on. You don't need to memorize any of this — the system applies it for you.
