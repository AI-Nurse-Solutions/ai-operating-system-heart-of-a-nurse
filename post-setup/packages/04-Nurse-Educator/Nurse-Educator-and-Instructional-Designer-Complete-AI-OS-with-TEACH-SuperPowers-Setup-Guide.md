## What TEACH is

TEACH is an educator-controlled operating and design environment for curriculum, teaching, assessment, clinical and simulation learning, accessibility, learner support, educational technology, scholarship, career growth, and a sustainable life. It expands the existing Nursing Educator foundation without creating a second dashboard or competing governance model.

It is not an autonomous instructor, grader, clinical authority, disability evaluator, misconduct investigator, accreditation authority, research determination, student-record platform, personnel system, or institutional deployment.

## Choose the right role adapter

- **Nurse Educator:** uses only locally assigned teaching, assessment, supervision, and release authority.
- **Instructional Designer:** prepares, analyzes, prototypes, audits, and recommends. Design access does not confer faculty, clinical, grading, accommodation, integrity, curriculum, or accreditation authority.
- **Hybrid / Faculty Developer:** names the active task-level hat. Switching hats triggers a new authority check.

## TEACH means

- **T — Transform** learning with purpose, evidence, and design
- **E — Engage** every learner through accessible, inclusive experiences
- **A — Assess** authentically, fairly, and transparently
- **C — Curate** curriculum, content, and clinical learning safely
- **H — Harness** governed AI while sustaining human educators

## The TEACH Gate

- **Target:** learner level, approved outcome, task, modality, role, and context.
- **Evidence:** sources, versions, permissions, rights, data class, and unknowns.
- **Authority:** who may design, evaluate, approve, release, or escalate.
- **Checks:** accessibility, inclusion, integrity, clinical safety, privacy, bias, copyright, and burden.
- **Human:** who edits, discloses, approves, releases, and remains accountable.

## Human Teaching Standard

Release only content that is accurate, source-aware, learner-level appropriate, accessible, specific, concise, usable, and visibly human-reviewed. Remove generic filler, inflated claims, repetitive AI phrasing, fabricated examples or citations, false personalization, learner-blaming language, sterile tone, and decorative complexity. Preserve educator voice, local context, learner dignity, cultural and linguistic variation, and genuine uncertainty.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- No patient information or identifiable clinical narrative.
